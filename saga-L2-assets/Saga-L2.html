Overview

The New Drago Family is a five-year franchise saga, an Italian-American crime-family drama set between the Caribbean, Santa Barbara, New York, Italy, and Montreal. It follows one of the most powerful families of its time and the people orbiting them as they navigate love, loyalty, crime, finance, and power in the 2000s and beyond.

The storyline is built on a semi-historical five-year chronology set in the 2000s: as the family succeeds, the pressure rises — and success itself brings challenges that threaten the family’s own existence.

Audience promise
A cinematic, sunlit world where glamour is real and consequences are darker.
Franchise logic
Recurring sets, evolving storylines, and a structure engineered for scale.

Characters and stakes

At its heart stands Don Domenico Drago: charismatic, feared by his enemies, and deeply human. Around him gravitate Lenny Abimonti, his brilliant consigliere whose intellect slowly outgrows his conscience; Margareta Otterra, the wise art expert who becomes an emotional compass as she navigates between art, love, and danger; and a vivid ensemble of heirs, allies, and rivals. Across the saga, these characters rise, fall, and reinvent themselves — discovering the cost of belonging.

Power

The empire expands, and every gain creates a new vulnerability.

Identity

Love and loyalty collide with ambition, secrecy, and legacy pressure.

Consequence

Financial crime, violence, and politics leave permanent marks.

Tone: Sunlit Noir

The saga’s world is “sunlit noir” reflecting the duality of the criminal mind: bright, glamorous settings where luxury looks real and the shadows feel sharper. Under sunlit beauty unfolds a wide spectrum of crimes, from elegant financial manipulations to brutal violence. Beauty and danger coexist, and success always carries a cost.

Beautiful skies and colors, sharp shadows, and a constant sense of threat beneath the surface.

Format and directors

The franchise is built as a complete structure: five two-part films and five TV seasons, for up to seventy hours of interconnected content. The films deliver major milestones and theatrical impact; the series explores intimate character evolution and the family ecosystem in greater depth. Two directors and showrunners share the vision to keep the duality in the look and rhythm evolving across the five-year arc.

Films
Event-level milestones designed for theatrical scale and impact.
Series
Psychological depth, relationships, and long-term consequence.

Production logic

Behind the scenes, the saga is engineered to achieve high-end quality while remaining financially intelligent. Recurring locations and durable story assets allow sets, costumes, and visuals to be reused and evolved rather than rebuilt from scratch. Each production blitz concentrates filming into a single intense year, generating efficiencies while maximizing creative consistency across multiple releases.

Reuse

Blitz model
Concentrated production for coherence and cost control.
