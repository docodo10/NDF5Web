Creative

Creative intent

NDF is not about glamorizing crime. It’s about exposing its contradictions. The saga explores the duality of the criminal mind: characters who can be generous, charming, and protective—then manipulative, ruthless, or dangerous when power is threatened.

These roles are built on moral tension rather than stereotypes. Characters justify their actions, defend values they struggle to apply to themselves, and navigate the gap between intention and consequence. Crime in NDF is not constant violence—it’s a shifting psychological state shaped by fear, loyalty, ambition, and denial.

For actors and creatives, NDF offers characters that evolve year after year, confronting the cost of their choices as personal power collides with social and geopolitical forces. It’s prestige storytelling designed to entertain, challenge, and leave a mark—without ever celebrating the crime itself.

Schedule

Schedule format

The project is designed for clarity, performance, and efficiency, subject to partner alignment.

Clarity

A defined target format: two major production windows (targets: 2028 and 2030), a planned break year, and role windows that can be mapped early.

Parallel format

The intent is to shoot the films and the series in parallel within each blitz, for efficiency, keeping availability and tone aligned.

Efficiency

The design favors a Montréal-based hub, reusable sets, and disciplined continuity to control costs and protect performance time on set, subject to producing partner decisions. Location work in New York, California, Italy, and the Dominican Republic is planned across the six titles to meaningfully reduce cost per minute.

Biweekly weekend travel

Scheduling is designed to support biweekly weekend travel options for key talent and HODs, after a certain period, subject to production partner policies and budget.

Creative leadership note for reps

Production model

Production Model & Workload Rationale

Workload is comparable to shooting roughly two seasons within one year.

Production Blitz 1 covers two story years, producing two two-part films and two premium series seasons, following the family year by year in depth instead of compressing full-year arcs into a single feature.

Films deliver the primary milestones (spring and early summer). The series expands causes and consequences, and character evolution (fall). Across PB1, the series totals 18 hours (two seasons of 12×45 minutes), alongside 10 hours of films.

Film and series are captured in parallel with the same cast, sets, lighting, and costumes, preserving a unified premium look. The series delivers about 75% original footage: roughly 14 of 18 hours come from TV’s own shooting (selectively integrating about 4 hours from the films, plus about 6 hours captured in parallel during film days, and adding about 8 hours of extensions and added scenes).

Why this format exists

The goal of the saga is to follow the Drago family with realism over five years, through a semi-historical chronology, delivering a truly immersive experience.

Parallel capture and premium standards

Films and series are planned to shoot simultaneously, not sequentially: same actors, same sets, same lighting, same production design. The dual format preserves authenticity: films for high-impact milestones, while the series adds causes, strategy, fallout, and the psychological pressure of power. The result is one coherent saga delivered as premium content in both formats.

Workload and manageability for A-list talent (≈ 2 Seasons)

Simultaneous capture concentrates the commitment into a defined production cycle. Instead of returning repeatedly over years, performers shoot once per cycle while generating multiple releases. In practice, it is comparable to shooting roughly two seasons within one year, followed by a one-year break and two years of releases across six titles.

Why two director/showrunner/crew teams in parallel

This volume cannot be carried by one director without sacrificing performance attention or coverage quality. Two director/showrunner teams operate in parallel under shared standards and continuity guardrails, keeping the scale premium, controlled, and actor-friendly: one team is most often aligned with the Dragos’ point of view, while the other is most often aligned against them (opposition, pressure, threats).

Value alignment

Two-year commitment & upside

Key talent is intended to be engaged across two major production windows (targets: 2028 and 2030), reflecting the scope of each blitz (roughly 28 hours / 1,680 minutes: two two-part films plus two seasons). Because the workload extends beyond a standard 2–3 hour feature, compensation is designed to scale accordingly. Competitive terms and meaningful participation are negotiated case-by-case and documented in the relevant agreements, subject to financing, partners, and customary deal structures.

“In practice, cast may experience the workload like shooting two premium seasons across a full production year — but the output becomes six releases, not one or two. Production Blitz 2 replicates the model in 2030. For the fifth year, a futuristic epilogue is planned, with an AI-assisted production approach, subject to full stakeholder approval.”

Process

How we work with reps

We prioritize a streamlined, professional workflow for agencies and management — with clear materials, fast feedback, and confidentiality when needed.

1) Request access

We share role briefs, arcs, and packaging context through NDA / controlled access.

2) Creative alignment

A short call to confirm tone, scope, and how the role evolves across entries.

3) Auditions & chemistry

Tapes, reads, and (when relevant) chemistry sessions — designed to respect time and availability.

4) Offers & scheduling

We aim for clarity on dates, travel, and role windows early — to protect other commitments.

Professional standards
