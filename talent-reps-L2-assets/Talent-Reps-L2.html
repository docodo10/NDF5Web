Creative

Creative intent

NDF is not about glamorizing crime. It’s about exposing its contradictions. The saga explores the duality of the criminal mind: characters who can be generous, charming, and protective—then manipulative, ruthless, or dangerous when power is threatened.

These roles are built on moral tension rather than stereotypes. Characters justify their actions, defend values they struggle to apply to themselves, and navigate the gap between intention and consequence. Crime in NDF is not constant violence—it’s a shifting psychological state shaped by fear, loyalty, ambition, and denial.

For actors and creatives, NDF offers characters that evolve year after year, confronting the cost of their choices as personal power collides with social and geopolitical forces. It’s prestige storytelling designed to entertain, challenge, and leave a mark—without ever celebrating the crime itself.

Schedule

Schedule format

The production plan is built for clarity, flexibility, and efficiency — with two concentrated production blitzes separated by a planned break.

Clarity

A defined format: two major shoots (2028-2030), a break year (2029), and role windows that can be planned early.

Parallel format

The plan is to shoot the films and the series in parallel within each blitz, for efficiency, keeping tone and availability aligned.

Efficiency

A Montréal hub, reusable sets, and disciplined continuity reduce friction and protect performance time on set.

Biweekly weekend travel

Production can arrange every-other-weekend travel for key talent and HODs.

Execution

How film + series run in parallel

Each production blitz targets roughly 1,000 minutes of screen storytelling by running feature and TV units in parallel, with early post flowing continuously at the hub.

Clear leadership lanes

The two franchise directors lead the overall creative vision. In TV, the showrunner-of-record and producing director execute in close coordination with them to keep tone, continuity, and delivery aligned across film and series.

One set, two deliverables

Core dramatic beats are captured once. Features prioritize premium “hero” moments, then the series shoots immediate extensions and connective tissue while the set-up is still live.

Parallel post, protected locks

Features and TV cut in parallel with dedicated teams. Feature picture locks and DI polish are protected for spring/summer releases, while TV locks follow for fall delivery.

Talent benefit

Fewer duplicate days, fewer re-setups, tighter continuity, and a clear chain of command — designed to protect performance while scaling output.

How we work with reps

We prioritize a streamlined, professional workflow for agencies and management — with clear materials, fast feedback, and confidentiality when needed.

1) Request access

2) Creative alignment
