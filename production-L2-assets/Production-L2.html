Structure

Blitz 1 Production Structure

Two tandems work in parallel, within a single production blitz, across two simultaneous story cycles to create 6 titles: 4 film parts and 2 TV seasons. Per cycle, the lead film directors generate about 4 hours of core narrative material that forms the shared spine of the two-part film and the 9-hour, 12-episode season. These film-foundation scenes are captured first, or given shooting priority, with the same actors, sets, lighting, costumes, and core camera team, while the showrunners and episode directors expand that base with about 5 additional hours of series-specific content. In total, this model produces about 8 hours of film narrative and 10 hours of additional series content, converting 18 hours of original narrative production into 26 hours of premium content.

Production comparison

The project delivers 18 hours of unique filmed material within a 12 month principal photography window, supported by dual units, Montreal-centered post-production, and structured A/A+ scene architecture. Comparable high-end series shoot 8–10 hours in 6–8 months; this model scales that production logic across simultaneous film and series formats but with two directors, showrunners, and crews.

Studio-facing advantages

Concentrated cycles increase deliverable volume per production window, stabilize release cadence, and strengthen packaging leverage across theatrical and premium streaming/TV. The model expands total franchise value without extending principal photography across multiple separate years.

Production Continuity and Premium Standards

Film scenes are produced first, not through full parallel film-and-series shooting. The same actors, sets, lighting, production design, and core camera crew carry across both formats, preserving a unified premium look. For selected moments, short series scene extensions are captured immediately after the corresponding film scene, while the director moves on to prepare the next film setup and the showrunner supervises the added series material. Additional series-only scenes are then produced in planned follow-on blocks. This model optimizes time, cost, and continuity while allowing the series to expand the film foundation with deeper character, connective tissue, and narrative consequence.

Schedule discipline and release runway

Post-production begins immediately during principal photography, with editorial, VFX, music and finishing running in parallel to support a rolling delivery schedule. Releases are then staged across the following two years, with six titles delivered on a controlled cadence.

Note

This structure is designed to stay premium while remaining operationally efficient: unified standards, centralized editorial/post, and clear canon decisions across film and series.

Standards

Consistency and quality control

The saga’s scale requires a framework that protects coherence, craftsmanship, and premium continuity across multiple titles, timelines, and creative teams.

Dual-director creative logic

The two film directors may shape complementary tonal registers across the saga. One can lean toward the Family’s internal world, emotional gravity, and point of view, while the other can lean toward external threats, adversaries, and danger — from the LocosColos to other foreign threats. This contrast can strengthen the project artistically, as long as it remains anchored in a shared story foundation and unified premium standards.

These tonal and stylistic choices are defined by the film directors, with the approval of the creator and, where applicable, the studio or streamer and key financing partners.

Creative spine

A shared story bible, robust character arcs, and clear production rules keep the universe coherent while still leaving room for interpretation, elevation, and contrast by key creatives, including the showrunners and episode directors.

Shared benchmarks

Common standards for tone, visual language, lighting, color, sound, and editorial rhythm help ensure that each title feels premium and unmistakably NDF, even when perspective, tension, and dramatic emphasis shift.

Delivery discipline

A centralized editorial and post-production pipeline, combined with clear creative approvals, supports staggered film and series releases without sacrificing consistency or craftsmanship.
