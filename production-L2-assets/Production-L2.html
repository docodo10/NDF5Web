Structure

Franchise production structure

Production Blitz 1 covers two story years, producing two two-part films and two premium series seasons. This structure follows the family year by year in depth, rather than compressing full-year arcs into a single feature.

Films deliver the primary milestones (spring and early summer). The series expands causes and consequences, and character evolution (fall). Across PB1, the series totals 18 hours (two seasons of 12×45 minutes), alongside 10 hours of films.

Film and series are captured in parallel with the same cast, sets, lighting, and costumes, preserving a unified premium look. The series delivers about 75% original footage: roughly 14 of 18 hours come from TV’s own shooting (about 6 hours captured in parallel during film days plus about 8 hours of extensions and added scenes), while selectively integrating about 4 hours from the films.

Studio-facing advantages

Concentrated cycles increase deliverable volume per production window, stabilize release cadence, and strengthen packaging leverage across theatrical and premium streaming/TV. The model expands total franchise value without extending principal photography across multiple separate years.

Parallel capture and premium standards

Films and series are planned to shoot simultaneously, not sequentially: same actors, same sets, same lighting, same production design. Dedicated TV coverage captures TV-specific material in parallel (extensions, connective tissue, added scenes), while film cameras focus on premium cinematic coverage. Film-originated material is integrated selectively only when performance, coverage, and technical standards strengthen the episodic cut.

Schedule discipline and release runway

Post-production begins immediately during principal photography, with editorial, VFX, music and finishing running in parallel to support a rolling delivery schedule. Releases are then staged across the following two years, with six titles delivered on a controlled cadence.

Note

This structure is designed to stay premium while remaining operationally efficient: unified standards, centralized editorial/post, and clear canon decisions across film and series.

Standards

Consistency and quality control

The saga’s format requires a system that keeps tone and craft aligned across multiple entries, units, and timelines.

Two directors + showrunners model

Two directors share the saga’s cinematic language and lead two film units to tackle the required volume while preserving a unified premium signature that reflects the duality of the criminal mind—each director bringing a distinct style: one rooted in the Family, the other in its oppositions.

Each director is paired with a showrunner who leads a complementary series unit, ensuring that the television entries maintain narrative depth, character development, and continuity with the films.

Film and series are produced at the same time across four units: the two film units drive master coverage and “canon” choices, while two lighter series units—under showrunner leadership—shoot longer dialogue beats and complementary scenes to deepen character and continuity.

A shared story bible, unified tone rules, and a centralized editorial/post pipeline keep everything aligned.

Creative spine

Robust bibles, arcs, and production rules keep the universe coherent while still leaving room for interpretation and elevation by key creatives.

Shared benchmarks

Common standards for tone, color, sound, and editorial rhythm help ensure each entry feels premium and unmistakably “NDF.”

Delivery discipline

A clear delivery approach supports release planning across film and series without sacrificing craftsmanship.
