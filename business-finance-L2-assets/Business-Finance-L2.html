Overview

Business logic

The New Drago Family unites A-list ensemble talent with premium production execution to deliver an original storyline. Built as a franchise asset, NDF is structured for scalable financing and commercialization: multiple premium titles produced under one unified plan, with recurring revenues and audience engagement over several years.

Scope & indicative scale (Production Blitz 1)

Six premium titles produced in one concentrated year (films + series seasons), supported by major production and distribution budgets for 6 releases over 2 years.

Value thesis

Long-form franchises can compound value through theatrical, streaming, home entertainment, and ancillary windows—while deepening audience retention with annual releases.

Capital efficiency

The production-blitz approach concentrates spend, reduces reset costs, and allows strategic reuse of premium assets—supporting consistent quality while controlling unit cost.

Guiding principle

Maximize early revenues (streaming, sponsorship, tax incentives), offering reasonable fees to talent in exchange for potentially high upside participation and alignment.

Alignment

Alignment and incentive design

The model aims to align stakeholders through reasonable cash compensation, structured deferrals, and performance-based upside—helping protect quality, support a premium ensemble, and manage early cash burn at PB1 scale, with final structures set in partnership with the producing studio.

Stakeholder logic

The project is built to be attractive to partners who think in lifetime value terms: a scalable franchise with multiple releases and sustained engagement, rather than a single swing.

    Financing

    Four-pillar financing strategy

    The base structure targets four complementary pillars, designed to reduce reliance on any single source while improving bankability and partner alignment.

    1. U.S. studio participation

    A studio anchor supports production leadership, distribution strategy, and credibility with lenders and global partners.

    2. Senior debt and production lending

    Senior facilities can be structured around contracted revenues and eligible incentives, with controls tailored to delivery requirements.

    3. Canada spend optimization

    To unlock meaningful incentives while leveraging world-class crews and infrastructure.

    4. Sponsors and strategic partners

    Brand partners and cross-media collaborations can contribute cash, in-kind value, or marketing leverage—without compromising creative integrity.

    Optional complements

    Depending on distribution choices, the structure may also include pre-sales, territory-based minimum guarantees, or streamer-linked advances—always assessed against long-term franchise value.
